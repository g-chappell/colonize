# The Order of the Kraken — Universe Reference

**Document purpose:** Single-source worldbuilding context for development agents (games, wiki, future narrative tools and fiction engines).
**Current in-universe date:** Year 2191 of the New World (NW 2191), within the **Early Liberty Era**.
**Last updated:** NW 2191 canon pass.

---

## 1. How to use this document

This document is intended to be self-contained and modular. Each top-level section can be queried independently. When passing context to an agent, consider passing only the relevant sections rather than the whole document.

### Canon tiers

Some statements in this document are firmly established; others are provisional or deliberately open. Where status matters, it is marked inline:

- **[ESTABLISHED]** — settled canon; treat as fact.
- **[DRAFT]** — working canon; subject to refinement. Agents may build on it but should not treat it as immutable.
- **[OPEN]** — deliberately unresolved. Do not invent canon to fill the gap; flag it back to the human author.

Where no marker is given, assume ESTABLISHED.

### Conventions

- Dates use the **New World (NW)** calendar, a continuous renaming of the pre-Collapse Old World (OW) calendar; NW 2191 corresponds to the year 2191 in the continuous reckoning. Pre-Collapse events may be referenced with either NW or OW dates interchangeably.
- Faction names, ship names, and place names are rendered in Title Case.
- "The Kraken" (capital T, capital K) refers to the specific Abyssal Horror; "a kraken" or "the kraken legend" may be used for folk-tale usage.
- **Mechanics vs flavour:** where a game-system agent finds mechanics implied but unspecified (e.g., how a Talisman grants protection), it should prefer not to invent mechanics and instead flag for the human. Narrative agents have more latitude.

## 2. High-concept pitch

The Earth is a water world. The last of humanity lives on floating man-made cities scattered across an endless ocean, scavenging the pre-Collapse technology that once promised the stars. Recovered space-capable tech now lets some ships sail the sky as well as the sea, and a new battle for universal dominance has begun. Piracy has been reborn as the dominant way of life.

Ancient bloodlines of legendary pirates — long thought extinguished — are being summoned back under the banner of the **Order of the Kraken**, devotees of the eldritch sea-god Kraken and inheritors of the lost knowledge of the Old World. The OTK sits within the **Crimson Tide**, one of three major alignments contending for the future of the species. The authoritarian **Azure Dominion** holds the deep, the sky, and much of the recovered tech. **The Forsaken** — smugglers, cultists, and rogue pirates — profit from the conflict or pursue their own ends.

Beneath all of it, the **Abyssal Horrors** — primordial leviathans sleeping in the deep — are stirring.

The setting is the **Early Liberty Era** of the New World, NW 2191.

## 3. The World

### Geography

The surface of the planet is a single unbroken ocean. No continental landmass is known to exist above water in the present day, though small islands — the peaks of former mountains — have recently been sighted for the first time in centuries. One such island is the location of the Archive.

Habitation divides into three domains:

- **Above the waves:** floating, man-made cities and island-cities, dotted across the endless ocean and connected by known maritime routes. This is where most of humanity lives.
- **Below the waves (the Deep / Davy Jones's Locker):** the dark ocean depths. Some of the pre-Collapse elite survived by retreating into airtight underwater bunkers, and their descendants still dwell there — now the heart of Azure Dominion territory. The Abyssal Horrors also slumber here. **[DRAFT]** The exact geography and political structure of the Deep is not fully mapped in current canon.
- **The sky / the heavens:** opened to humanity only recently, via salvaged pre-Collapse space technology. Sky-capable ships now contest the upper reaches. **[OPEN]** Specific propulsion, range, and operational mechanics of sky ships are not yet defined.

### Technology

Technology is hybrid and salvaged. A typical pirate vessel blends old-world wooden hull craft, rigging and cannon with recovered pre-Collapse components — avionics, power cells, weapons systems. Factions differ sharply in tech level:

- **Azure Dominion** has the greatest access to pre-Collapse technology and actively advances it (FTL research, genetic engineering, advanced weaponry).
- **Crimson Tide** and the **OTK** rely more on salvaged tech and ship-craft passed down from the Golden Age, augmented where possible.
- **Ironclad Syndicate** controls much of the industrial and manufacturing base and supplies all sides.

### Notable environmental phenomena

- **The Rayon Passage** — the known sea route connecting the chain of floating cities; the spine of civilisation.
- **The Red Tide** — a vast crimson expanse of ocean that emits a barely-visible shimmering mist during daylight hours. The mist is lethal; prolonged exposure causes suffocation. The mist subsides at dusk. Folk tales describe the Red Tide as the hunting ground of the Kraken — "a fly to a spider's web." **[DRAFT]** Whether this is literal or metaphorical is unresolved.
- **Fata Morgana** — mirages common in the far waters beyond the final floating cities. Some are illusions that have lured ships to their doom; others have resolved into real landmasses.
- **Emerging islands** — the first reports of land above the waves in centuries. Long dismissed as drunken fantasy, now confirmed by the Endeavour's discovery of the Archive.

## 4. Timeline & Eras

All dates are in the continuous NW/OW calendar. The present day of the setting is **NW 2191**, within the Early Liberty Era.

### 1. Prosperity Era — NW 1400–1800 (≈400 years)

The Golden Age of Piracy. The legendary pirate captains who found the thirteen bloodlines are active. The Order of the Kraken exists in secret, preserving ancient knowledge and ties to the Abyssal Horrors, waiting for the right moment to act. The pirate way of life becomes romanticised.

**Active factions:** OTK (hidden); Founding Pirate Families.

### 2. Corruption Era — NW 1800–2100 (≈300 years)

Industrialisation. Environmental degradation, political corruption, vast inequality. The Azure Dominion elite — foreseeing collapse — begins constructing underwater outposts and secret Archives to safeguard themselves and their technology. The OTK remains hidden. The first Phantom Corsairs emerge.

**Active factions:** Azure Dominion (elite phase, hidden); OTK (hidden); Phantom Corsairs (emergence).

### 3. Collapse Era — NW 2100–2150 (≈50 years)

Floods, freak storms, tsunamis, seismic upheaval. Global civilisation falls. The seas rise until a single ocean covers the world. Ocean-faring folk adapt above; the privileged seal themselves into their submerged bunkers; most of humanity perishes. The Azure Dominion consolidates in its underwater fortresses. The OTK re-emerges to guide the Crimson Tide's survivors. The Pale Watch forms to guard Abyssal sites from human interference.

**Active factions:** Azure Dominion (retreat); OTK (re-emergence); early Crimson Tide (Blackwater Collective, Bloodborne Legion); Pale Watch (emergence).

### 4. Liberty Era — NW 2150–2550 (≈400 years)

Rebuilding. Societies above the waves re-form around floating cities. The Crimson Tide coalesces under OTK guidance. Later sub-eras see the rise of the Ironclad Syndicate and the Abyssal Brotherhood.

- **Early Liberty Era — NW 2150–2250** ← **current setting**
  - Crimson Tide expanding. OTK guiding fleet-building and recovery of lost tech. Pale Watch active.
  - The Archive has recently been discovered by Captain James Sparrow and the crew of the Endeavour (NW 2191).
- **Mid Liberty Era — NW 2250–2350**
  - Ironclad Syndicate emerges as neutral industrial power. Abyssal Brotherhood emerges as cult-like faction.
- **Late Liberty Era — NW 2350–2500**
  - Ironclad Syndicate consolidates. Abyssal Brotherhood grows more aggressive.

### 5. Ascension Era — NW 2500–2700 (≈200 years)

The Azure Dominion resurfaces in response to Abyssal attacks and instability. The Crimson Tide challenges the Dominion for control of Earth's remaining resources. Ironclad Syndicate profits from both sides. Abyssal Brotherhood grows bolder.

### 6. Retribution Era — NW 2700–2900 (≈200 years)

Conflict between Azure Dominion and Crimson Tide peaks. The Abyssal Horrors awaken in full force, attacking all factions. The Pale Watch attempts to contain the chaos; the Phantom Corsairs thrive on instability.

### 7. Wrath Era — NW 2900–3100 (≈200 years)

Earth is ravaged by war and Abyssal attacks. The major factions prepare to leave Earth. Abyssal Horrors run free; the Pale Watch fights a losing holding action.

### 8. Exodus Era — NW 3100–3400 (≈300 years)

Humanity leaves Earth. Fragile space colonies form. The Ironclad Syndicate secures its position as a key industrial power in the new frontier. The Abyssal Brotherhood continues its pursuit of Abyssal power. The Pale Watch follows humanity into space.

## 5. Factions

Three broad alignments organise the political landscape of the Early Liberty Era.

### Crimson Tide (Red alignment)

*Egalitarian, rebellious, independent. Drawn from the spirit of piracy, freedom, and resistance against authoritarian control.*

- **Order of the Kraken** — the spiritual and historical heart of the Tide; see §6 for deep dive.
- **Blackwater Collective** — smugglers, ex-mercenaries, and rogue traders. Provide resources and intelligence through underground supply routes; sabotage Dominion operations.
- **Bloodborne Legion** — the disciplined military arm of the Tide. Former soldiers, pirates, and revolutionaries. Lead direct assaults on Dominion forces to liberate oppressed territories.
- **Scarlet Forge** — the Tide's engineers. Craft advanced weaponry and heavily modified ships, repurposing abyssal-infused materials to strengthen the fleets.
- **Sons of Scylla** — guerrilla fighters who worship the Abyssal Horror Scylla. Multi-pronged ambushes, naval tactics; strike unpredictably and swiftly like their patron.

### Azure Dominion (Blue alignment)

*Autocratic, elitist, control-focused. Maintain dominance through technological superiority and strict hierarchy.*

- **Astral Covenant** — the Dominion's scientific arm. Focused on space travel and ancient technology; developing faster-than-light travel.
- **Cerulean Knights** — elite warriors trained to perfection, equipped with cutting-edge technology. Uphold the Dominion's order, protect the elite, suppress rebellions.
- **Sapphire Enclave** — technocratic faction dedicated to the recovery and manipulation of ancient technologies. Provide the Dominion with advanced tools, weaponry, and genetic engineering capabilities.
- **Sovereign Guard** — the primary military force. Maintain order across vast territories with advanced fleets and battalions.
- **Imperial Veil** — covert operations. Espionage, sabotage, intelligence gathering. Keep the Dominion's enemies divided through manipulation and subterfuge.

### The Forsaken (Neutral alignment)

*Survivors, traders, rogue factions, and Abyssal worshippers operating outside the influence of the Tide and Dominion.*

- **Abyssal Brotherhood** — cult-like faction worshipping the Abyssal Horrors as a pantheon. Believe the horrors hold the key to ultimate power; conduct rituals to harness it.
- **Ironclad Syndicate** — powerful industrial faction controlling resources and manufacturing hubs. Trade with both sides while securing their own strongholds.
- **Pale Watch** — guardians of Abyssal sites. Ensure ancient creatures remain undisturbed. Intervene only when necessary to prevent catastrophe.
- **Phantom Corsairs** — rogue pirates who refuse alignment. Prey on both Tide and Dominion supply lines. Thrive on chaos; avoid open conflict through stealth and unpredictability.

### Faction relationships at a glance

- Crimson Tide ↔ Azure Dominion: open enmity.
- Ironclad Syndicate: trades with both, aligns with neither.
- Pale Watch: treats all other factions as potential threats to Abyssal containment.
- Abyssal Brotherhood: hostile to the Pale Watch; opportunistic otherwise.
- Phantom Corsairs: hostile to both Tide and Dominion logistics; may briefly ally with anyone for the right score.

## 6. The Order of the Kraken (deep dive)

### Identity

The OTK is the spiritual and historical heart of the Crimson Tide. It is defined by bloodline: its members are descendants of the thirteen legendary pirate captains of the Old World's Golden Age of Piracy.

The OTK is presented — and perceived — in two overlapping ways, both canonical:

- **Internally** (and in their own Archive texts): the noble order of ancient pirate bloodlines; guardians of the Old World's lost knowledge; blessed and protected by the Kraken; heirs to the Golden Age; custodians of the Pirata Codex. "Here be dragons" — *we are the dragons*.
- **Externally** (particularly in Azure Dominion propaganda and frightened tavern-talk): Kraken-devotee zealots whose raids are swift and merciless, aimed at unleashing chaos and bringing down the Dominion. Marked by the tentacle and the dragon; touched by the abyss.

Both framings describe the same organisation. Which one is reached for depends on who is speaking.

### Tenets and heraldry

- **Bloodline.** Membership flows through the thirteen pirate families; see §7.
- **"Hic sunt dracones"** — "here be dragons." The phrase and the paired-dragon motif (two dragons facing each other) serve as the Order's mark; they appear on maps, weapons, and regalia. In the Old World, cartographers wrote *hic sunt dracones* to warn of dangerous unknown seas. The OTK adopted the phrase as a warning **about themselves.**
- **The Kraken Talisman.** A mystical token that binds its bearer to the Order and is said to offer protection against the Kraken itself. 250 Talismans were distributed to the surviving crew of the Endeavour following the discovery of the Archive.
- **The Archive.** The Order's primary source of recovered Old World knowledge, located on the newly-rediscovered island and still being catalogued.

### The "Hic sunt leones" splinter

**[DRAFT]** At some point in the Order's history, a splinter faction attempted to infiltrate and subvert the OTK, adopting *hic sunt leones* ("here be lions") as their banner. They are described in Archive texts as "outside forces" — the implication being not true descendants of the bloodlines. The mutiny was "quickly and ruthlessly snuffed out," but whether every leonine trace was eradicated is unclear. **[OPEN]** Whether a leonine faction persists underground, and whether it could be an antagonist vector, is an open design question.

### The Order's current phase (Early Liberty Era)

Following the Endeavour's return from the Archive:

- The Elders have received documents from the Archive containing the full pirate family trees and crests, making bloodline identification possible for the first time in centuries.
- A call has gone out across the Rayon Passage to gather members of the thirteen bloodlines.
- Under the guidance of the 250 surviving Talisman-holders, plans are being drawn up for a larger expedition back to the Archive.
- A fleet is being assembled. Seasoned captains and crew are being recruited.
- The Order intends to honour the legendary ships of the Golden Age by recreating them with modern technology — see §9.

### Known symbols

- Two dragons facing each other.
- The phrase *hic sunt dracones* in text.
- The Kraken as rendered in OTK Archive art: skull-like head, octopus tentacles, dragon's body.
- The Kraken Talisman (visual design **[OPEN]**).

## 7. The Thirteen Bloodlines

The thirteen pirate captains of the Old World whose descendants form the OTK. **[ESTABLISHED]** Ching Shih was historically the last to be formally added to the roster; older texts may refer to "the Twelve," but thirteen is current canon. **[OPEN]** Whether further bloodlines could be recognised in future is not decided.

Each entry below is drawn from the OTK Archive's recovered biographical fragments. These are the *historical* figures; their modern descendants are not yet individually named in canon.

### 1. Anne Bonny

One of the most notorious female pirates. Born in Ireland around 1700, moved to London and then to the Province of Carolina at about ten years old. Around 1718 she married the sailor James Bonny, took his name, and moved with him to Nassau in the Bahamas — a sanctuary for pirates. There she met Calico Jack Rackham and became his pirate partner and lover. Red-haired, considered a "good catch," with a fiery temper; a formidable foe.

### 2. Edward "Ned" Low

A notorious pirate of English origin, active in the early 18th century. Commanded small fleets of three or four ships. His crews captured at least a hundred vessels and burned most of them. One of the most vicious pirates of the Golden Age. The circumstances of his "death" around 1724 have been the subject of much speculation; sightings were recorded long after his ship was apparently lost at sea.

### 3. Bartholomew Roberts ("Black Bart" / "The Great Pyrate")

Welsh. The most successful pirate of the Golden Age of Piracy, taking over 400 prizes. Raided off the Americas and the West African coast. Created his own *Pirata Codex* (the pirate code) and adopted an early variant of the Skull and Crossbones flag. His infamy earned him the name *The Great Pyrate*, and later *Black Bart*.

### 4. Benjamin Hornigold

English. Operated during the tail end of the Golden Age. Began his pirate career in 1713, attacking merchant ships in the Bahamas. Helped establish the "Republic of Pirates" in Nassau. Captain of the Ranger, one of the most heavily armed ships in the region.

### 5. John Rackham ("Calico Jack")

English. Operated in the Bahamas and Cuba during the early 18th century. Nickname from the calico clothing he wore; "Jack" a nickname for "John." Most remembered for his two female crew members, Mary Read and his lover Anne Bonny. The flag associated with Rackham — a white skull above crossed swords on a black field — is thought to be the progenitor of the Jolly Roger.

### 6. Mary Read

English. One of the two most infamous female pirates of the 18th century (alongside Anne Bonny), and among the few women convicted of piracy at the height of the Golden Age. In 1720 she joined Calico Jack's crew, dressing as a man alongside Bonny. Her reign was swift but legendary.

### 7. Edward Teach ("Blackbeard")

English. Operated around the West Indies and the eastern coast of Britain's North American colonies. Captured the French slave ship *La Concorde*, renamed her *Queen Anne's Revenge*, equipped her with 40 guns, and crewed her with over 300 men. Nickname from his thick black beard; reported to have tied lit slow matches under his hat to frighten his enemies.

### 8. Emanuel Wynn

French. Began his career raiding English merchantmen off the Province of Carolina in the late 17th century. Later moved to the Caribbean, attacking English and Spanish ships. Famously escaped a 32-gun British fifth-rate frigate (commanded by Captain John Cranby) by slipping out of a cove at Brava Island. His flag paired the skull-and-crossbones with an hourglass — signifying to his prey that their time was running out.

### 9. William Kidd

Scottish. Commissioned as a privateer, turned pirate in practice. Legend holds that he was tried and executed in London in 1701 for murder and piracy, after capturing a French ship under an English captain. The belief that he left buried treasure greatly contributed to the growth of his legend.

### 10. Thomas Tew ("The Rhode Island Pirate")

English. 17th-century privateer turned pirate. Embarked on two major pirate voyages; met a bloody death on the second. Pioneered the route that became known as the Pirate Round. Claimed as one of the founders of the mysterious pirate colony of Libertatia. His personal standard: a white arm holding a short sword on a black field.

### 11. Charles Vane

English. Operated in the Bahamas at the end of the Golden Age. Commanded a number of ships as one of the leaders of the Republic of Pirates in Nassau. Known for cruelty — beating, torturing and killing captured sailors. In February 1719 he was caught in a storm in the Bay Islands and marooned on an uncharted island. Accounts of his subsequent capture by a passing British ship are vague; he was "rounded for slipping the noose."

### 12. Stede Bonnet ("The Gentleman Pirate")

Barbadian. Born into a moderately wealthy land-owner family before turning to the sea. Unusually for a pirate, he contracted a local shipyard to build him a sixty-ton sloop — which he equipped with 10 guns and named *Revenge* — rather than seizing a ship by mutiny or boarding. Had no prior knowledge of shipboard life. Later partnered with Blackbeard and unleashed a campaign of terror during the Golden Age.

### 13. Ching Shih

Chinese. Operated in the South China Sea during the early 19th century. Commanded one of the largest pirate fleets in history and established a code of conduct for her crew. Began her career as a prostitute in Guangzhou; taken captive by the pirate Cheng I, she became his wife and partner in piracy. After his death she took control of his fleet — some 300 ships and up to 40,000 men — and expanded through alliance with other pirate leaders to a fleet eventually exceeding 1,500 ships.

## 8. The Abyssal Horrors

Primordial leviathans sleeping in the deep. They embody the sea's primordial chaos and predate human civilisation. The major factions relate to them in sharply different ways:

- **OTK** and **Sons of Scylla** each venerate one Horror specifically (Kraken and Scylla respectively) and draw identity and purpose from that patron.
- **Abyssal Brotherhood** worships the full pantheon as a cult and seeks to harness their power through ritual and experiment.
- **Pale Watch** guards their slumbering sites and intervenes to prevent anyone — cultist, Dominion scientist, or Crimson Tide raider — from disturbing them.
- **Most other groups** fear them and avoid known Abyssal zones.

**[DRAFT]** The nature of the Horrors — whether they are truly supernatural entities, prehistoric megafauna, biotechnological remnants, or something stranger — is deliberately ambiguous in current canon.

### The pantheon

- **Cthulhu** — The Great Dreamer, who slumbers beneath the ocean's abyss. Brings madness and ruin upon awakening.
- **Behemoth** — A massive, armoured titan. Causes cataclysmic earthquakes and tsunamis; embodies the combined fury of earth and sea.
- **Kraken** — A colossal tentacled beast that drags entire fleets into the depths. Symbol of chaos and destruction. **The OTK's patron Horror.** In OTK Archive art the Kraken is depicted with a skull-like head, octopus tentacles, and the body of a dragon. Folk tales place the Kraken as the hunting force behind the Red Tide.
- **Leviathan** — A vast sea serpent that controls the tides. Capable of apocalyptic devastation through tidal waves and destruction.
- **Scylla** — A many-headed monster known for swift, multi-pronged attacks. Embodies chaotic unpredictability and terror. **Patron of the Sons of Scylla.**
- **Cetus** — A colossal sea monster that swallows entire cities. Embodies the raw, uncontrollable power of the ocean.
- **Charybdis** — A monstrous whirlpool that consumes ships and drags them into the abyss. Represents unstoppable natural forces.
- **Bahamut** — A massive dragon with shimmering scales. Worshipped as a god of order and destruction; capable of levelling cities with celestial fire.
- **Typhon** — A primordial dragon of storms and chaos. Summons catastrophic tempests that destroy entire civilisations.
- **Hydra** — A many-headed serpent that regenerates endlessly. Symbol of undying destruction and insatiable hunger for devastation.

## 9. Legendary Ships

Six ships recovered from Archive records as the most legendary of the Old World's Golden Age. The OTK intends to honour each by rebuilding them — old silhouette, new technology — as the flagships of the new fleet.

### Queen Anne's Revenge

Originally the French slave ship *La Concorde*, seized by Blackbeard in 1717 and refitted as his flagship. Famously fast — able both to run down her prey and outpace pirate-hunters. Rumoured to carry at least 40 cannons. Legend has it that Blackbeard himself scuttled her when finally outmatched, rather than let her fall into another's hands.

### The Black Pearl

Details sparse even in the Archive. A grand ship, recognisable by her black hull and black sails. Reputed untouchable for her speed, due to an unusually large area of sail. Originally known as the *Wicked Wench*; per legend, burned and sunk, then resurrected from the sea floor by Davy Jones himself and renamed. Said to be faster than the Flying Dutchman, and weighed down only by the "curse of the Black Pearl." **[OPEN]** The curse is not elaborated in the Archive.

### The Flying Dutchman

Per Archive records, one of the most notorious pirate ships of all. Later European nautical myth deemed her a ghost ship destined to sail forever, appearing to sailors as a herald of catastrophe — her hull reportedly glowing with a ghostly light. Recovered fragments give her specifications as 420 tons, 170 feet stern to bow, 46 cannons plus 2 double-barrelled chase guns. Considered the fastest ship both on and beneath the sea, if the legends are to be believed.

### The Whydah

Originally a slave ship, captured on one of her voyages by pirates under "Black Sam" Bellamy. Became one of the most successful treasure-hunting ships of the Golden Age. Per the records, her last voyage ended when her crew — drunk after yet another successful run — ran her aground. She carried 28 cannons. The treasure her crew had taken from over 50 ships was only partly recovered when the wreck was found.

### The Ranger

A 30-gun brig. Captained at various times by Benjamin Hornigold and Charles Vane, and at one time or another hosted Blackbeard, "Black Sam" Bellamy, and Stede Bonnet. Her legendary status was sealed under Calico Jack, who made her a ship to be feared by all who crossed her.

### The Revenge

A sloop. Originally armed with 10 guns, upgraded to 12, crewed by 70. Not remarkable for firepower — remarkable for a knack for slipping into and out of ports on smuggling runs so quietly that no one knew she had been there. Captained by Stede Bonnet; paired with the Queen Anne's Revenge during Bonnet's partnership with Blackbeard.

## 10. Key Locations

- **Port Royal** — Hub floating city. Starting point for the Endeavour's expedition to the Archive. Home to the Tavern where OTK rumours and recruitment percolate. Starting locus for much narrative material.
- **Tortuga** — Floating city. Hosts the Black Market where Kraken Talismans have been known to be illegally traded. Rough. Opportunistic.
- **Barataria Bay** — The last floating city on the Rayon Passage before the frontier; beyond it lies unknown water. Origin of the "marked men" — Barataria Bay natives bearing the dragon-mark tattoo, whose significance is discussed under §12.
- **The Rayon Passage** — The known sea route connecting the floating cities. The spine of civilisation in the present day.
- **The Archive** — A capsule of Old World history embedded in the rock face at the top of what was once a mountain, now an island of green amid the blue. Discovered by Captain James Sparrow and the crew of the Endeavour in NW 2191. The first confirmed land sighting since the Collapse. Primary source of recovered OTK knowledge, family trees, Pirata Codex references, and ship blueprints. Still only partially explored.
- **The floating cities (general)** — Man-made, typically named with echoes of Old World maritime and pirate geography. **[OPEN]** A fuller gazetteer is not yet established.
- **The Deep / Davy Jones's Locker** — The submerged realm. Includes the Azure Dominion's underwater fortresses (descended from the pre-Collapse elite bunkers), Abyssal Horror slumbering-sites, and the dark quiet of unmapped ocean floor.
- **The Red Tide** — A vast crimson expanse of ocean with lethal daylight mist. Folklore names it the hunting ground of the Kraken. See §3.
- **Libertatia** — A pirate colony founded (per legend) by Thomas Tew. **[OPEN]** Status in the New World — whether it survives, whether it has analogues in the current floating-city network — undecided.

## 11. Characters of note

### Captain James Sparrow (deceased, NW 2191)

Young captain of the Endeavour. Led the expedition that rediscovered the Archive — the first confirmed land since the Collapse — and whose return sparked the current revival of the OTK. Died of wounds sustained during the Endeavour's wreck on the island itself; his diary is preserved as primary source material and is the principal first-person account of life in the current era. Credited by the Order as the figure "who relit the flames of the OTK."

### The Endeavour's crew

- **Mr. Jackson** — First Mate. Iron-willed disciplinarian. Maintained order through much of the voyage using a cat-o'-nine-tails. Instrumental in preventing mutiny during the storm, the doldrums, and the Red Tide.
- **Mr. Paulson** — Navigation Officer. Lost during the five-day storm in the mid-voyage; swept overboard by a freak wave.
- **Mr. Woodhall** — Ship's Doctor. Tended to the ill through the fog and the Red Tide; attended Sparrow in his final hours.
- **Mr. Johnson** — Quartermaster.
- **The 250 survivors** — Of the original complement (~320 at departure from Port Royal, last reinforced at Barataria Bay), 250 survived to return home. Each was rewarded with a Kraken Talisman on their return to the Rayon Passage.
- **The Barataria Bay contingent** — Frontier folk taken on at the last floating city before the unknown. Several bore a tattoo resembling a double-headed dragon or two dragons facing each other. During the Red Tide episode, they demonstrated unexplained immunity to the lethal mist. A group of them were subsequently thrown overboard by crewmates driven mad by fear, as "sacrifice to the Kraken." **[OPEN]** The full significance of the Barataria Bay mark, and whether it indicates OTK initiation, is undetermined; see §12.

### Supporting figures

- **The Elders** — A council of senior figures across the floating cities, particularly in Port Royal and Barataria Bay. Custodians of what tradition and memory remain. They authorised the Endeavour's expedition, received the Archive documents on its return, and now coordinate the gathering of the thirteen bloodlines.
- **The Stranger in Shadows** — An unnamed figure Sparrow encountered in the Port Royal tavern before the voyage, whose words "struck deeper" than the usual tavern whispers and compelled Sparrow to assemble the Endeavour's crew. **[OPEN]** Identity unknown; possibly an OTK emissary, possibly something stranger.
- **Sparrow's successor** — **[OPEN]** Command of the 250 survivors and the expedition's legacy passes to someone after Sparrow's death. Not yet named in canon.

## 12. Artifacts, Symbols & Iconography

### The Kraken Talisman

A mystical token marking its bearer as OTK. Offers protection (folk belief specifies protection from the Kraken itself). 250 were distributed to the surviving Endeavour crew after their return. Some have since been lost in drunken games of chance, fenced on the Black Market in Tortuga, or drunk away in taverns — each such loss a minor scandal in OTK circles. **[OPEN]** The Talisman's physical appearance and the mechanics of its "protection" are not defined.

### The OTK Crest and motto

- **Two dragons facing each other** — the visual mark, engraved on recovered weaponry and regalia.
- **"Hic sunt dracones"** — the motto, used either alongside the paired-dragon motif or on its own.
- Both derive from Old World cartographic practice (dragons marking dangerous unknown waters), inverted by the Order to mean *we are the danger*.

### The Dragon Mark tattoo

Worn by members of the Barataria Bay contingent aboard the Endeavour. Depicted as a double-headed dragon, or two dragons facing each other. The bearers dismissed it as acquired "on a rum-fuelled night in the Tavern." During the Red Tide episode those who bore it demonstrated immunity to the lethal mist that killed non-bearers. **[OPEN]** Relationship between the Dragon Mark tattoo and the OTK crest (paired-dragon motif) is suspiciously close but not formally established. Open design question whether the mark is an OTK initiation sign or a separate, older tradition that the OTK has incorporated.

### The Kraken (iconographic)

OTK Archive art depicts the Kraken with a skull-like head, octopus tentacles, and the body of a dragon — a composite that pulls together the three folk traditions (octopus, squid, dragon) into one image.

### "Hic sunt leones"

Banner of the historical splinter/infiltration mutiny within the OTK. "Here be lions." Officially suppressed; status as a surviving threat is **[OPEN]**.

### The Pirata Codex

The pirate code created by Bartholomew Roberts. References to it appear in the Archive. **[OPEN]** Full text not yet recovered or adapted as OTK constitution; potential design vector.

### The Jolly Roger (and bloodline variants)

The iconic Jolly Roger derives from Calico Jack's flag (white skull above crossed swords on black). Each of the thirteen bloodlines historically flew their own variant flag; these are being reconstructed as OTK heraldry. Known distinctive variants:

- **Emanuel Wynn** — skull and crossbones with an hourglass (time running out).
- **Thomas Tew** — white arm holding a short sword on black.
- **Bartholomew Roberts** — an early variant of the Skull and Crossbones.
- Others **[OPEN]** or **[DRAFT]**.

### The undeciphered cipher

A string that appears repeatedly across the recovered Archive texts. The repetition signals importance; the meaning is unknown:

> *Cb Ks mz Vyrvglis, rvi fj lry Pdfukif ei rzoxp jbu xhz hlvefxve cihkw bi elg hlvefxve cihkw?*

**[OPEN]** Do not invent a plaintext. Treat as a narrative hook.

## 13. Tone, voice & themes

Guidance for narrative, copy, and fiction-engine agents. Mechanics agents can skip this section.

### Core mood

Weathered romance of piracy, meeting eldritch dread of the deep, against a backdrop of salvaged-futurism where recovered Old World tech runs alongside wooden hulls and rigging. Three registers coexist and should be audible at different intensities:

- **The salt-and-rum register** — taverns, songs, Calico Jack in calico, the cat-o'-nine-tails, rum rations.
- **The eldritch register** — mist, fog, the shadow beneath the waves, the Red Tide, Kraken dreams, "unseen eyes watching from beneath."
- **The salvaged-futurism register** — recovered avionics, the sky-reaches, the Archive as a time capsule, the Azure Dominion's FTL research, the idea that the species once almost reached the stars.

### Themes

- **The sea as living force** — beautiful, cruel, indifferent. Grants and takes without reason or remorse.
- **Rediscovery** — every generation sifts myth from fact; truth salvaged piece by piece from Davy Jones's Locker.
- **Bloodline and inheritance** — who you are is partly who your ancestors were. The thirteen bloodlines literalise this.
- **Freedom vs control** — Crimson Tide's rebellious egalitarianism vs the Azure Dominion's autocratic elitism.
- **The return of the repressed** — Abyssal Horrors beneath everything, stirring; what was buried does not stay buried.
- **Hope in ruin** — survival above all, with sparks of new ambition. Sparrow's epilogue is the template emotional note.

### Voice

- **Narrative prose** — weathered, first-person or close-third. Diary-style is canon for character entries (Sparrow's voice is the reference). Long sentences and nautical imagery are welcome.
- **Dialogue** — seafaring idioms ("by my count," "Davy Jones's Locker," "rum-soaked tongues," "hands brushing their own small charms"), period-inflected but not pastiche. Not mock-Shakespearean; not modern-slang.
- **Descriptions** — sensory, oceanic, atmospheric. Fog, mist, crimson tide, lantern-light. Time of day matters — dawn and dusk are often threshold moments.
- **Danger** — the Archive uses the cartographic register (*hic sunt dracones*) when marking danger. Narrative can borrow this for chapter headers, warnings, interstitial text.

### Reference style artifacts

For narrative agents needing style calibration:

- Captain Sparrow's Diary (entries 1–16) and the Captain's Epilogue — the canonical first-person voice.
- Archive fragments — cooler, reconstructive, present-tense.
- Tavern rumour — unreliable, embellished, second-hand.

### Anti-patterns

- Avoid full modern-internet diction in narrative voice.
- Avoid making the eldritch register comic; the Kraken is not a mascot.
- Avoid resolving **[OPEN]** items by invention; flag them.
- Avoid reducing the OTK to either pure heroes or pure cultists; the dual framing is the point.

## 14. Open questions & canon gaps

Explicit list of items flagged as **[OPEN]** or **[DRAFT]** above, collected for quick reference. Agents should not resolve these without consulting the human author.

1. **The cipher.** *"Cb Ks mz Vyrvglis…"* — meaning unknown; do not fabricate a plaintext.
2. **The Hic Sunt Leones splinter.** Officially suppressed; whether it persists underground is undecided.
3. **Sky navigation mechanics.** How sky-capable ships work — propulsion, range, relation to sea vessels — undefined.
4. **The below-the-waves society.** Its geography, politics, and relationship to the Azure Dominion elite bunkers are thinly drawn.
5. **Exact founding of the OTK.** "Ancient" — precise origins unknown.
6. **Kraken Talisman mechanics.** The form the "protection" takes; physical appearance of the Talisman.
7. **Dragon Mark tattoo.** Relationship to the OTK crest. Whether Barataria Bay bearers are OTK initiates or a separate, possibly older, tradition.
8. **The Stranger in Shadows.** Identity unknown.
9. **Sparrow's successor.** Command of the 250 survivors passes to someone; not yet named.
10. **Further bloodlines.** Whether any 14th (or later) bloodline could be recognised in future.
11. **Libertatia's status in the New World.** Survived? Analogue in current floating-city network?
12. **The Curse of the Black Pearl.** Referenced in Archive but not elaborated.
13. **The Red Tide's literal nature.** Folklore names it the Kraken's hunting ground; whether this is literal, metaphorical, or wrong is unresolved.
14. **Abyssal Horrors' ontological status.** Supernatural, prehistoric, biotech, or stranger — deliberately ambiguous.
15. **A fuller gazetteer of floating cities** beyond Port Royal, Tortuga and Barataria Bay.
